Habitat:

The Flopper or Life Plant can be found in open woodland, woodland gaps, path edges, waste ground, disturbed areas, lawns and roadsides.

Characteristics:

This plant growing to 6 feet or 2 metres tall has rather succulent stems and leaves. Individual plants vary tremendously in size but even very small ones will flower. The leaves may be single or compound, each leaflet being about 2 1/2 inches or 6.5 centimetres long and 1 1/8 inches or 4 centimetres in width and quite thick and fleshy from top to bottom. Those leaves at the tip of a leaf-stalk are larger. The leaves are light-green with a reddish edge. The plant may flower profusely with numerous flowers which hang down. These flowers which are about 1 1/4 inches or 3 centimetres in length, are tubular and greenish-purple in colour at first but when they are fully mature, bright orange-red, pointed petals just project from the end. They flower from spring into early summer. The Flopper is an introduced plant which has become naturalized. It is Native to Asia.
