Habitat:

This shrub is mostly found in open, dry, sandy woodland where considerable sunlight reaches the forest floor. It also occurs in the open on inland slopes and has been widely planted in parks and nature reserves.

Characteristics:

This is a shrub up to 8 feet or 2.4 metres in height. It can grow into a small tree in other areas. The branches are slender and upright or leaning slightly. The leaves which are 2-4 inches or 4-8 centimetres long are a very elongated and oval with smooth edges. They are light green to yellowish in colour, with a pointed tip and a very short leaf stalk. They grow quite densely together towards the branch tips. The flowers are not showy and appear in close groups in leaf axils near to the branch tips. They are yellowish-white in colour and hang down like an inverted bowl. The flowering seasons are spring and summer. The seed pod is characteristic and has four membranous wings. These give it the common name of Hopseed Bush used elsewhere. It is native to Bermuda and is also native in Florida, Cuba and Jamaica as well as in tropical, subtropical and warm temperate regions of Africa, southern Asia and Australasia. It is widespread in Bermuda and common in places.
