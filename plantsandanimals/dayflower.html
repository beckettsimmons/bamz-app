Habitat:

The Creeping Day Flower is a low-growing plant found in marshes and other damp locations as well as on open ground.

Characteristics:

This is a soft-textured, creeping vine which forms large, dense mats in damp places. The stems are brittle and 1-3 feet or 35-100 centimetres long, they creep along the ground rooting from the nodes. The leaves are a bright, light green and 1-3 inches or 2.5 to 5 centimetres long. They are candle-flame shaped with a somewhat blunt tip. The leaf surface may be either smooth or covered in small hairs. The flowers occurring in small clumps are very light blue and small, but quite charming. This plant produces flowers over most of the year. The seeds are capsules with two compartments, each with 5 brown seeds. It is native to Bermuda having arrived well before man. It is also common in tropical America and the Caribbean islands. This plant is used as a medicinal herb in China to bring down fever and to treat kidney problems.
